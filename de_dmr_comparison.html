Methylation–Expression Concordance: DMRs vs Differential Expression

Introduction

Differential methylated regions were discovered between the hyper and hypo sepsis phenotypes. The question is how these regions relate to an entirely independent bulk RNAseq dataset made up of sepsis patients who are also labeled as hyper or hypo inflammatory.

Differential expression was performed with both DESeq2 and limma voom since there is some belief that DESeq2 has inflated FDR in large population studies. That doesn’t seem to be the case for this data, and DESeq2 and limma voom nearly entirely agree. I recommend using the deseq lfc and padj for analysis.

The hypothesis is that methylation suppresses expression. Thus, genes with higher differential methylation in Hyper patients (positive dmr_beta) should have lower expression in Hyper relative to Hypo (negative or near-zero log2FC), and vice versa. This predicts a negative Spearman correlation between DMR mean beta and DESeq2 log2FC.

Data dictionary

This is the dataframe shared with this notebook, which is an inner join (only gene symbols in both the unfiltered DE results and unfiltered DMR results) between the unfiltered differential expression results (both DESeq2 and limma-voom), and the unfiltered DMR regions (from the genomic ranges obj). For the DE results, I recommend using the deseq lfc and padj. The results of the two are very nearly identical, however.

Methylation vs expression

A Spearman correlation of ρ = 0.054 (p = 0.0494, n = 1301) between DMR mean beta and DESeq2 log2FC indicates a positive monotonic relationship. A significantly negative ρ would support the methylation-suppresses-expression hypothesis; the observed result does not support that model.

DMR region classification

Each of the 2289 DMRs was classified using distance to the nearest gene body (TxDb hg38, 35,025 genes). - intragenic if the DMR overlaps a gene body (distance = 0) - intergenic otherwise.

Methylation-expression correlation by DMR class
DMR class N Spearman rho p-value % expected
intergenic 264 0.051 0.4130 55.7
intragenic 1037 0.054 0.0833 46.5

Neither class shows a meaningful expected signal. Both intragenic and intergenic DMRs have a small positive ρ, and neither approaches significance in the expected (negative) direction. The intergenic class has a slightly higher proportion of expected-direction gene pairs (55.7%) than the intragenic class (46.5%), but the difference is modest and not reflected in the correlation.
